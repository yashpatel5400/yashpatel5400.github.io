01 Mission

The design loop is humanity’s core technology.

Since the dawn of humanity, one ability has distinguished us from every other species on Earth: our unique ability to conceive and build tools. From the Wright Brothers tinkering on Kitty Hawk to Edison chipping away at light bulb designs, every machine we’ve ever built has come out of the same loop — propose an idea, test it against nature, iterate.

While the process has been systematized over the centuries with a better understanding of physics and better computer tooling, the core loop has remained unchanged. Today, “testing against nature” means running a numerical PDE solver — hours to days to estimate a single quantity, like drag or stress, for a single design. This is true whether you’re designing an airplane wing, a fusion tokamak, or the qubits of a quantum computer.

Zoom out: engineers are generators of ideas, and nature, captured by these simulations, is the verifier. Computers can potentially generate candidate ideas orders of magnitude faster than humans — but they’re bottlenecked on the same side we are.

03 Approach

Neural surrogates, made reliable.

An ML surrogate pays its cost once, in training, and from then on evaluates designs in a single inference pass — analogous to the speedup wrought by AlphaFold over molecular dynamics. The catch has always been that these surrogates fail precisely where we most care about them: on the new designs we’re trying to discover in the first place. Fixing that is the whole point of this company.

01 / Uncertainty

Uncertainty-aware design

Estimate the errors a surrogate is expected to make, then generate designs that are robust to them. Novel proposals stay trustworthy — even far from anything the model has seen before.

02 / Hybrid

Hybrid classical–neural solvers

Interleave classical numerical methods with the surrogate on the parts of the problem that demand them. Retain correctness guarantees where they matter, while keeping the efficiency gains everywhere else.

03 / Active

Active data generation

Generate new training samples exactly where the surrogate is weakest, and incrementally improve the solver. The loop tightens itself.
